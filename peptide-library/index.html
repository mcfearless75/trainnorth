How grading works

Four tiers. A compound being widely sold does not raise its grade.

GradeCountMeaning
Approved medicine8Approved by a recognised medicines regulator somewhere in the world.
Human trial data3Human trial evidence exists and is not hedged, but the compound is not approved.
Limited human data14Some human evidence, explicitly hedged, unreplicated, or largely mechanistic.
Preclinical only11Animal or mechanistic evidence only, with no completed human trials.

Grading is deliberately negation-aware: an evidence note reading “no human trials” or “not yet approved” is resolved as a denial before any positive match is considered, because those phrases contain the exact wording a naive classifier would match on and would otherwise invert the grade.
