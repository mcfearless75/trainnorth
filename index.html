01 Protagonist
Who is keeping this record

A 46-year-old with a notebook and no sponsor.

He runs, eats, trains and lives to get healthier and further in control of his own life, and writes all of it down. There is no supplement brand behind this, no coaching funnel at the end of it, and no before-and-after photograph taken in different lighting.

It started as fat loss. Two years of reading turned it into something longer. The compounds worth knowing about at forty-six are mostly not the ones that shift the scale. They are the ones that affect sleep quality, tendon repair, insulin sensitivity, immune function and how well you recover from the training you can still do. Weight is simply the easiest thing to measure, which is why the internet measures almost nothing else.

The record exists because the fitness internet is built on outcomes without method. Someone posts a result; the working is missing. What is published here is the working: the sessions, the weeks where nothing moved, and the research that informed the decisions, with its limitations stated first rather than buried.

Where laboratory compounds appear in this record, they appear as documentation and reference material. They are not recommended, not sold from this site, and not presented as a shortcut.

The method ↓
02 Method
AI-assisted training

The plan is written after the session, not before it.

Loads, energy, sleep and pain go into a session journal. The next day is built from what actually happened, not from a twelve-week template bought off someone with better genetics.

01

Session memory

Each session is scored against the last one for the same movement, so progression is measured rather than assumed.

02

Full-set progress

Back-off sets and volume count, not just the top single. Most plateaus are visible in the sets nobody posts.

03

Daily conditions

Energy, joint pain and available equipment change the session. The plan adapts to the day rather than overriding it.

04

Reusable prompt

The full prompt is published. Copy it, point it at your own journal, and run the same method without the paywall.

05 Reference
36 compounds · evidence graded

A reference library, not a shop window.

Eight categories, and only one of them is weight. Recovery and tissue repair, growth hormone axis, metabolic, cognitive, sleep, dermal. The compounds that matter for staying capable are spread across all of them.

Every entry carries an evidence grade on the card: approved medicine, human trial data, limited human data, or preclinical only. Eight of the thirty-six are approved medicines somewhere in the world. The other twenty-eight are not, and the library says so before it says anything else.

Reference data is only half of it. The other half is arithmetic people get wrong.

06 Tooling
Reconstitution reference

Milligrams in. Units on the barrel out.

The most common error in this field is not compound selection. It is the conversion between a vial strength, a diluent volume, and what that means on a U-100 syringe. This calculator does that arithmetic and shows the draw.

Reference dose points are drawn from the published research range for the selected compound. They are shown for reference only and are not a recommendation to administer anything.

08 Questions
Straight answers

What this is, and what it is not.

Is any of this medical advice?

No. Everything published here is personal documentation and general education. It is not diagnosis, not treatment, and not a protocol. Speak to a qualified physician before making any health decision.

Is this an online shop?

No. Nothing is sold from this site and there is no payment process on it. This is an educational record and a reference library. Fulfilment, where it happens at all, is handled entirely by an independent supplier.

What does the referral code actually get me?

Five percent off, priority processing and priority shipping with the independent supplier. It is a referral arrangement and is disclosed as one. Quote the code in your enquiry.

How does an enquiry work?

You select the research materials you are interested in, this site helps you assemble a clear enquiry, and you send it to the independent supplier yourself. No payment, no stock and no dispatch happens here.

Why does the library grade evidence so bluntly?

Because the grade is the most useful thing on the card. A compound with strong rodent data and no human trials should not look identical to an approved medicine, and on most sites it does.
